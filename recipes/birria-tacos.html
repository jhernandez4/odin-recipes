Birria Tacos

Description

There are tacos, and then there are tacos. You can really put anything in a corn tortilla and call it a taco, and sure, it's delicious. But if you had to make a ranking of the best, the tastiest and most succulent, the birria tacos would be in first place, or at least in the top three. Birria is a slow-cooked meat stew, which results in a bouillon and delicious meat that melts just by looking at it. Although birria is traditionally made with lamb, it has tended to evolve towards veal (beef) birria, and is now the most typical way to prepare it. You can use many parts of the veal, but the oxtail contributes its characteristic deep flavor, and results in a very sweet and juicy filling. The stew, as such, can be eaten alone. But stuffed into tortillas, by a healthy amount of Oaxacan cheese, and then seared in the pan with the resulting fat from the stew… well, that's just another roll. And if later you take that taco and spread it in the tasty and slightly spicy consommé…. I'm salivating just thinking about it.

Ingredients

Beef Birria

  • 9 ripened tomatoes
  • 5 ancho chiles
  • 8 guajillo chiles
  • 2 chipotle chiles in adobo
  • 1 onion
  • 7 garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon allspice
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon of Mexican oregano (or normal)
  • Half a cinnamon stick (pref. Ceylan)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 600 ml of water
  • 35ml white vinegar
  • 1.5 kg of oxtail
  • 1 liter of beef broth

Birria Tacos

  • beef birria
  • birria broth
  • Oaxaca cheese
  • corn tortillas
  • sunflower oil
  • 1 sweet onion
  • fresh coriander
  • 3 limes

Steps

Beef Birria

  1. Remove the stems from the tomatoes and cut them in half. Cut the onion into large pieces.
  2. Remove the stems from the guajillo and ancho chiles, then scoop out the seeds.
  3. Peel the garlic cloves.
  4. In a large saucepan add the black pepper, Jamaican pepper, cinnamon stick and cumin grains. Toast them over medium heat until fragrant.
  5. Add the tomatoes, onion and garlic cloves. Add the oregano. Sauté everything for 5 minutes.
  6. Add the tomato paste, ancho chiles, guajillo chiles, and chipotle chiles. Sauté for 4 more minutes.
  7. Add the water and white vinegar. Bring it to a boil and cook everything for 10 minutes.
  8. Transfer all solids to a food processor, along with 1 tablespoon of the cooking water. Blend for 2 minutes, smooth and smooth. book it.
  9. Cut the steak into medium pieces. Place a saucepan over high heat until very hot, add sunflower oil sear the meat in batches, until deep golden brown on all sides.
  10. Add the beef broth. Degrease the bottom. Add the chili and tomato sauce (adobo), and then water until it reaches the edge of the saucepan. Add a pinch of salt. Mix everything well and cook for 2 and a half hours, stirring from time to time so that it does not stick.
  11. After 2 and a half hours, take out the beef ribs, remove the bones and cartilage and then chop it finely. book it.
  12. An hour later (3 hours and a half of cooking) take out the oxtail, separate it from the bone and cartilage and chop it finely. book it.
  13. Add salt to the broth to taste, and a little sugar to correct the acidity if necessary.
  14. Sieve the bouillon through a strainer, and then reserve it in the fridge for a minimum of two hours.
  15. Take it out of the fridge and remove the layer of fat that will have formed on the surface. Reserve the fat for later use.

Birria Tacos

  1. Shred the Oaxaca cheese and reserve it. Chop half a sweet onion and a handful of coriander leaves.
  2. Heat a little of the consommé in a saucepan.
  3. In a frying pan, add a tablespoon of the broth fat along with a little sunflower oil. Mix them and heat them over medium heat.
  4. Put two pancakes on the fat. Brush them with a little broth and add meat to one of the halves of each tortilla. Put oaxaca cheese on top of the meat. With a spatula, fold the tortillas and set them aside, then repeat with more tortillas, adding fat and oil each time.
  5. 2 minutes after folding the tortillas, turn them over and fry them on the other side for two more minutes. Once ready, transfer them to a plate.
  6. On the same plate, place a container with the hot broth, and finish it off with some of the chopped sweet onion, the chopped coriander leaves and the juice of half a lime. Spread the taco, and eat as soon as possible.
